This page displays the factors that are taken into consideration when calculating loan approval decisions.

CIBIL Score: A credit score that evaluates an individual's creditworthiness, influencing loan approval decisions.

Income Level: The applicant's income is a key factor in determining the loan amount they can repay.

Loan Amount: The total loan amount requested by the applicant, affecting approval chances based on income and credit score.

Debt-to-Income Ratio: This ratio shows the applicant's ability to repay the loan in comparison to their existing debts.

Education Level: Higher education levels may suggest better employment opportunities and increased repayment capacity.

Marital Status: Whether an applicant is single, married, or divorced, as it can reflect financial stability or risk.

Dependents: The number of people the applicant is financially responsible for, affecting disposable income and repayment ability.

Self-Employed: Indicates whether the applicant is self-employed or salaried. Self-employment may be considered higher risk.

Luxury Asset: Refers to assets of high value, such as luxury cars or properties, which may influence loan eligibility based on collateral.

Residential Asset: Residential properties are common forms of collateral in loan applications and affect loan approval decisions.

Commercial Asset: Properties or assets used for business purposes, which may help secure larger loans depending on their value.
